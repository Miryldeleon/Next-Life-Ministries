WHAT WE BELIEVE

What We Believe

Rediscovering the life and message of Jesus through a broader spiritual and historical lens.

We believe understanding Jesus' life and teachings can be enriched by engaging multiple historical and spiritual sources with humility, discernment, and reverence. Our aim is not confusion, but clarity through a fuller view of the spiritual record.

The Bible

The Bible remains the foundational text of Christian faith. It grounds believers in the core narrative of creation, covenant, the life of Jesus, and resurrection hope. We believe revisiting scripture in historical context can deepen trust and illuminate meaning.

Nag Hammadi Library

Discovered in 1945, these texts reveal dimensions of early Christian diversity and highlight themes of inner awakening, spiritual knowledge, and transformation. We do not treat them as replacements for scripture, but as important historical witnesses.

Image Placeholder

Dead Sea Scrolls

These manuscripts offer crucial background on the religious world preceding and surrounding Jesus. They show scripture as a living tradition and help anchor biblical faith within a vibrant historical setting.

Image Placeholder

Ethiopian Bible

As one of the oldest and most complete Christian canons, the Ethiopian Bible preserves texts such as Enoch and Jubilees. Its witness reminds us that the early Christian world was wider, richer, and more varied than many realize.

Image Placeholder

The Urantia Book

We view the Urantia Book as a modern interpretive resource that some find spiritually meaningful for cosmology, spiritual progression, and the life of Jesus. While not part of the traditional canon, it is explored here as a complementary text for thoughtful seekers.

Together, these voices invite deeper exploration

When read with wisdom and discernment, these sources invite believers and seekers to pursue a more expansive understanding of God, the mission of Jesus, and the ongoing spiritual journey of life.

Next Life Ministries is dedicated to expanding understanding of the life, teachings, and spiritual message of Jesus through scripture, discovery, and faithful spiritual inquiry.
